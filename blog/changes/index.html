Changes coming

Over the next two weeks we are introducing some changes small but important changes to our hosting and support.

Domains

Currently we allow naked (apex / root / bare) domains to be used with Mixture hosted projects, this comes with some additional complications and overhead with our setup and hosting structure and has led to a couple of small instances of downtime.

We’ve decided it’s better for all of our users if we remove this feature, this will improve the stability of our hosting for everyone.

Don’t worry, you will still be able to use your domain with Mixture hosted projects, but instead of pointing at the ip address, you will point at the project name via a CNAME.

A CNAME record does not require hard-coded IP addresses. However, CNAME records are not available at the zone apex and can’t be used to configure root domains.

If you are using a domain it will be necessary to make this change as soon as possible.

You simply need to access the DNS settings for your domain and point the domain at your Mixture project as per the example below…

www.your-domain.com    CNAME    project-name.username.mixture.io

Support

We receive a lot of support requests, the majority of this support is not related directly to Mixture but more the technologies around it or a users specific setup, we’re always happy to help and we do our best but it’s not possible for us to answer every single question.

Due to this we’ve decided to take a more community driven approach and move support over to a Google group and actively encourage users of Mixture and the technologies to get involved.

We’re planning to introduce these changes over the next two weeks.

If you have any questions please email us at team@mixture.io.
